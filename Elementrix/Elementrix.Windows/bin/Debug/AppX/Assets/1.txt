Hydrogen
Hydrogen is the lightest element on the periodic table. Its monatomic form (H) is the most abundant chemical substance in the Universe. Non-remnant stars are mainly composed of hydrogen in its plasma state. The most common isotope of hydrogen, termed protium, has one proton and no neutrons.
